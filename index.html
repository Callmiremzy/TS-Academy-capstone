How Planetary Data Helps Us Understand Space

Planetary science goes beyond images. Comparing mass, diameter, gravity, and density, we gain insight into how planets form, behave, and interact within the solar system.

Visualizing the Differences Between Planets

Each planet in our solar system has unique physical characteristics. Visual comparisons help highlight how vastly different terrestrial planets are from gas giants and ice giants.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the solar system, with a rocky surface and extreme temperatures.

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet due to its thick atmosphere, and it is similar in size to Earth but covered with toxic clouds.

Earth

Earth is the only known planet that supports life, with water, air, and a suitable temperature for living things.

Mars

Mars is called the Red Planet because of its dusty surface, and it may have once had water and conditions for life.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet, made mostly of gas, and it has a giant storm called the Great Red Spot.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful rings and is a gas giant with many moons orbiting around it.

Uranus

Uranus is an icy giant that rotates on its side, making it unique, and it has a cold atmosphere with faint rings.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun, known for its deep blue color and strong winds and storms.
